Tips

  • Master OPVL: Practice origin, purpose, value, and limitation with a wide range of sources.
  • Plan Essays: Always create a thesis and plan before writing comparative essays.
  • Use Dates & Evidence: Support every argument with precise dates, names, and historical facts.
  • Understand Themes: Know major themes like conflict, revolution, independence, leadership, etc.
  • Link to Criteria: Practice Criterion A-D specifically for source analysis, investigation, structure, and reflection.

Past Paper Tips

  • Read the question twice to identify key directives and time periods.
  • Use structure: Introduction, 3 body paragraphs (each with evidence), and a clear conclusion.
  • Source questions: Link origin and limitation to the historical context.
  • Compare perspectives: Contrast sources or arguments to show depth.
  • Write within time: Practice writing full essays in 45 minutes or less.
